Adrenergic Agonists (Sympathomimetics) 
‚Ä¢ Epinephrine (Adrenaline): Hits Alpha-1, Beta-1, and Beta-2
  Indications:
    1. Anaphylactic Shock: Raises BP, opens airways
    2. Cardiac Arrest: Beta-1 jump-starts the heart
    3. Status Asthmaticus: Strong Beta-2 bronchodilation
  NCK Alert:
    - High-alert IV drug, requires pump
    - Monitor cardiac + BP
    - Side effects: Tachycardia, hypertension, anxiety

‚Ä¢ Norepinephrine (Levophed): Strong Alpha-1, some Beta-1
  Indication: Septic shock
  NCK Alert: Risk of extravasation ‚Üí tissue necrosis
    - Monitor IV site
    - Antidote: Phentolamine

‚Ä¢ Salbutamol (Ventolin): Selective Beta-2
  Indication: Asthma, COPD (rescue inhaler)
  Teaching:
    - For attacks only
    - Side effects: tremors, tachycardia
    - Frequent use = uncontrolled asthma

Part 2: Parasympathetic System (Cholinergic Drugs) 
‚Ä¢ Cholinergic Agonists (Press Brake / Make Wet)
  SLUDGE mnemonic:
    - Salivation
    - Lacrimation
    - Urination
    - Diarrhea/Diaphoresis
    - GI Cramping
    - Emesis
  Also: Bradycardia, Bronchoconstriction, Miosis

  Drugs:
    - Bethanechol (urinary retention)
    - Pilocarpine (glaucoma)

  Toxicity: Cholinergic Crisis
    - Antidote = Atropine

‚Ä¢ Anticholinergics (Block Brake / Make Dry)
  Drugs: Atropine, Ipratropium
  Mnemonic: "Can't See, Pee, Spit, St..."
    - Tachycardia, dry mouth, constipation, mydriasis
  Indications:
    - Atropine: Bradycardia, pre-op secretions
    - Ipratropium: COPD bronchodilation
